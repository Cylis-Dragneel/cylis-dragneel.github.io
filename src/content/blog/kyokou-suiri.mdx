# Review

<Callout type="info" title="After you read">
I do recommend checking out my notes section so you can see my descent into this rabbit hole as well as more of my deeper thoughts on what happens in the story as well as some of the smaller interactions as I won't mention everything in my review.
</Callout>

I originally came to this show with little to no expectations on what I was in store for, while the premise did intrigue me at the start it was the characters and their dynamics that swept me from the floor. The one thing that really caught me off guard is how our main protagonist Iwanaga Kotoko just has zero chill and no filter on her. Like every episode she'll be making dirty jokes or saying how she wants to eat Kuro's banana <Emoji src="https://cdn.discordapp.com/emojis/998735492105388132.webp?size=32" alt="ICANT" /> These characters and their interactions are what make most of the show imo, now don't get me wrong that doesn't mean the story isn't good as well but the same characters without these characters coupled with their seriousness and goofyness wouldn't be worth the watch.

The OST, especially the main theme was a banger. Both openings and endings peak. Especially the endings with the jazz songs just scratch that itch for me.

Now, I love myself a mystery anime. Hell I daresay mystery is my favorite genre of all time in media and this anime does a great job executing the mystery aspect while also leaving clues for you as the viewer to come to your own conclusions. In/Spectre is about (in the most non-spoilery way) Iwanaga Kotoko and her boyfriend Kuro solving cases regarding spirits, spectres and the supernatural thus came the name In/Spectre because it is a pun on Inspector <Emoji src="https://cdn.discordapp.com/emojis/1069833934453624863.webp?size=32" alt="frieren3" />..  yes. Throughout all of their cases you really feel the sense of curiosity overtake you. There were many a times that I was just awake at 3am because I couldn't stop until the arc was over, except the first season screwed me over when it came to this since it was basically just 2 arcs so I finished s1 in two nights

<Callout type="warning" title="Spoilers Ahead!">
I need to preface the following portion of the review saying there will be spoilers so I am forcing anyone who wants to read this to watch the series cause it is peak <Emoji src="https://cdn.discordapp.com/emojis/998464222956621824.webp?size=32" alt="garf" />
</Callout>

The real charm of In/Spectre comes with the absolute bullshit Iwanaga concocts just to satisfy the curiosity of the viewers and the people involved. The best example is her meeting with the giant snake where she literally lies that the girl dumped the fetus of her dead child who she had with her ex-boyfriend who supposedly cheated on her and did a double suicide with a girl but no he was framed and killed so she killed the killer who confessed to her after he thought his life was getting ruined because of karma but no actually she lied that she killed him to protect the younger brother of her ex-boyfriend who actually did the killing cause he had a future when she didn't. When it was actually just that, yea she killed him because he framed and killed her ex-boyfriend and that the snake misheard her saying "I hope they find you" but was too prideful to admit he misheard. Absolute cinema. It was at this moment that she had me in her grasp and I was thoroughly hooked on this show.

The existence of Kuro who is part human, part mermaid, part kudan who is immortal and can choose one of the most probably futures that will happen whenever he dies and then his cousin who is also his first crush and has the same powers but are on opposite sides because one has accepted their monstrous being while the other wants to fucking create a GOD and turn herself back into a normal human is certainly interesting <Emoji src="https://cdn.discordapp.com/emojis/998464222956621824.webp?size=32" alt="garf" /> Then we have Iwanaga who is smart as shit because she is the Goddess of Wisdom for the yokai because they kidnapped her when she was 11 and told her she should sacrifice an eye and a leg to become their goddess of wisdom to which she said yes with the brightest smile is just hilarious. Like when she is rediscovered people think some sick twisted kidnapper did that to her but no she just happily agreed to it with no coercion at all. Like they politely requested her while bowing and that's all it took lmao

All in all, this was a solid show where I had fun every bit of the way with I'd say no episode or moment feeling like a drag/waste of time. It was great to the point that I actually updated my score as I am writing this review and thinking back on everything that happened. If anything's a testament to how good the show is, I gave a brief intro to my sister about this anime similar to the first part of my review after she saw me watching it and as soon as I finished it she asked me to watch it together so I started my rewatch the very day I finished Season 2 (although that rewatch hasn't finished due to a mismatch of schedules)
**Score: 9.5/10**
